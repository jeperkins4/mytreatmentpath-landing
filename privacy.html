Privacy Policy

Last Updated: February 18, 2026

Privacy-First Design: MyTreatmentPath stores all your personal health information locally on your computer. We do NOT collect, transmit, or store your Protected Health Information (PHI) on our servers.

1. Our Privacy Philosophy

MyTreatmentPath is built on a simple principle: your health data belongs to you, and only you.

Unlike cloud-based health apps, we:

  • ✅ Store all PHI locally on your device
  • ✅ Encrypt your data with a password only you know
  • ✅ Never transmit your medical records to our servers
  • ✅ Give you full control over backups and data export
  • ✅ Make analytics participation completely optional

2. Information We Do NOT Collect

MyTreatmentPath does NOT collect, store, or transmit the following from your device:

  • ❌ Your name, email, phone number, or contact information
  • ❌ Medical diagnoses, test results, or health conditions
  • ❌ Genomic data or genetic test results
  • ❌ Medication lists or supplement regimens
  • ❌ Doctor names, hospital visits, or treatment history
  • ❌ Personal research notes or journal entries
  • ❌ Browsing history within the app
  • ❌ Device identifiers or location data

3. Local Data Storage

3.1 Where Your Data Lives

All personal health information is stored in an encrypted SQLite database on your computer at:

  • macOS: ~/Library/Application Support/MyTreatmentPath/data/health-secure.db
  • Windows: %APPDATA%/MyTreatmentPath/data/health-secure.db
  • Linux: ~/.config/MyTreatmentPath/data/health-secure.db

3.2 Encryption

Your database is encrypted using AES-256 encryption with a password-derived key (PBKDF2 with 10,000 iterations). This means:

  • Even if someone steals your computer, they cannot read your data without your password
  • We (MyTreatmentPath developers) cannot decrypt or access your data
  • If you forget your password, your data is permanently unrecoverable

3.3 Backup Responsibility

Because your data lives only on your device:

  • You are responsible for backing up your data
  • Use the App's "Export Data" feature or your OS backup tools (Time Machine, Windows Backup)
  • We cannot recover lost data if your device is damaged or lost

4. Optional Analytics (Opt-In Only)

Analytics is OFF by default. You must explicitly enable it in Settings.

4.1 What Analytics Collects (If You Opt In)

If you choose to participate, we collect de-identified aggregate statistics only:

Data Type What We Collect What We DON'T Collect
Demographics Age range (18-29, 30-39, etc.), State, Gender (optional) Name, exact age, city, ZIP code
Diagnosis Cancer type counts (e.g., "50 users with bladder cancer") Individual diagnoses, dates, stages
Mutations Mutation frequency (e.g., "30% have FGFR3 mutation") Individual mutation profiles, test results
Treatments Treatment popularity (e.g., "40 users researching checkpoint inhibitors") Individual treatment plans, doses, outcomes
Usage Number of articles bookmarked, app version Specific articles read, browsing behavior

4.2 HIPAA Safe Harbor De-identification

Analytics data is de-identified using HIPAA Safe Harbor standards (45 CFR §164.514(b)(2)), which removes all 18 identifiers:

  1. Names
  2. Geographic subdivisions smaller than state
  3. Dates (except year)
  4. Telephone numbers
  5. Fax numbers
  6. Email addresses
  7. Social Security numbers
  8. Medical record numbers
  9. Health plan numbers
  10. Account numbers
  11. Certificate/license numbers
  12. Vehicle identifiers
  13. Device identifiers
  14. URLs
  15. IP addresses
  16. Biometric identifiers
  17. Full-face photos
  18. Any unique identifying number/code

4.3 Minimum Cell Size

We apply a minimum cell size of 11 individuals to all aggregated data. This means:

  • If fewer than 11 users share a characteristic (e.g., "BRCA1 mutation in Alaska"), that statistic is suppressed
  • This prevents re-identification through combination of attributes
  • It's a higher standard than HIPAA's minimum (which allows 3+ in some cases)

4.4 How Analytics Works

  1. Once daily (at 2:00 AM local time), the app generates aggregate statistics from your local database
  2. These statistics are de-identified using Safe Harbor rules
  3. Aggregates are sent to our Supabase PostgreSQL database (hosted on AWS)
  4. Individual records are NEVER transmitted—only counts and percentages
  5. You can view exactly what's being shared in Settings → Analytics Dashboard

4.5 Why We Collect Analytics

Aggregate data helps us:

  • Understand which mutations and treatments are most commonly researched
  • Improve research article recommendations
  • Identify gaps in medical research databases
  • Contribute to broader scientific understanding of patient needs

4.6 Opting Out

To disable analytics:

  1. Open Settings → Privacy
  2. Toggle "Participate in Anonymous Analytics" OFF
  3. No data will be transmitted (even aggregate statistics)
  4. App functionality is unchanged

5. Research Article Access

When you search for research articles, the App queries public APIs (PubMed, Europe PMC). These queries:

  • Do NOT include your personal health information
  • Use generic search terms (e.g., "FGFR3 bladder cancer treatment")
  • Are sent over HTTPS (encrypted in transit)
  • May be logged by third-party APIs (PubMed, NIH) for their own analytics

We do NOT track which articles you read or bookmark. That information stays on your device.

6. Cookies and Tracking

MyTreatmentPath does NOT use cookies, trackers, or analytics on this website.

Our marketing website (mytreatmentpath.com) does not use:

  • Google Analytics or similar tracking
  • Advertising pixels (Facebook, Twitter, etc.)
  • Third-party cookies
  • Behavioral tracking or fingerprinting

Downloads are served directly from GitHub Releases. GitHub may log download IPs for abuse prevention.

7. Third-Party Services

MyTreatmentPath uses these external services:

7.1 PubMed / Europe PMC (Research Articles)

  • Purpose: Fetch research article metadata
  • Data shared: Search keywords only (no PHI)
  • Privacy policy: NIH Privacy Policy

7.2 Supabase (Analytics Database)

  • Purpose: Store de-identified aggregate analytics (if opted in)
  • Data shared: De-identified counts and percentages only
  • Hosting: AWS (SOC 2 Type II certified)
  • Privacy policy: Supabase Privacy Policy

7.3 GitHub (App Distribution)

  • Purpose: Host downloadable installers
  • Data shared: Download IP (logged by GitHub for abuse prevention)
  • Privacy policy: GitHub Privacy Statement

8. Children's Privacy

MyTreatmentPath is not intended for children under 13. We do not knowingly collect information from children. If you are a parent/guardian and believe your child has used the App, please contact us.

9. Data Retention

9.1 Local Data (PHI)

Your encrypted health data remains on your device until you:

  • Manually delete it using the App's "Delete All Data" feature
  • Uninstall the App and delete its data folder

9.2 Analytics Data (De-identified)

If you've opted into analytics:

  • Aggregate statistics are retained indefinitely for research purposes
  • Opting out stops future data transmission but does NOT delete past aggregates (they're already anonymized)
  • You can request deletion by contacting support@mytreatmentpath.com

10. Data Breach Notification

Because your PHI is stored locally and encrypted:

  • We cannot be breached for your data (we don't have it)
  • If de-identified analytics data is breached, we'll notify users within 72 hours
  • Because analytics data is de-identified, re-identification risk is minimal

11. Your Rights

You have the right to:

  • Access: View all data stored locally (use the App's "Export Data" feature)
  • Correction: Edit any information in the App
  • Deletion: Delete all data via Settings → Delete All Data
  • Portability: Export data to JSON format for use elsewhere
  • Opt-out: Disable analytics at any time (Settings → Privacy)

12. International Users

MyTreatmentPath is developed in the United States and complies with:

  • HIPAA (USA): Safe Harbor de-identification for analytics
  • GDPR (EU): Data minimization, consent, right to deletion
  • CCPA (California): Transparency, opt-out, data deletion

If you are outside the US and opt into analytics, de-identified data is stored on AWS servers in the US.
